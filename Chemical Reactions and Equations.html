📝 Assertion & Reason Questions (1 Mark)

A1. (31/1/1)

Assertion (A): Decomposition reactions are generally endothermic reactions.
Reason (R): Decomposition of organic matter into compost is an exothermic process.

A2. (31/2/1)

Assertion (A): Different metals have different reactivities with water and dilute acids.
Reason (R): Extraction of a metal from its ore depends on its position in the reactivity series.

A3. (31/2/1)

Assertion (A): Silver chloride turns grey in sunlight.
Reason (R): Decomposition of silver chloride into silver and chlorine takes place by sunlight.

A4. (31/2/2)

Assertion (A): Sodium, calcium and magnesium are obtained by electrolysis of their molten oxides.
Reason (R): These metals have more affinity for oxygen than carbon.

A5. (31/2/3)

Assertion (A): Sodium oxide is an amphoteric oxide.
Reason (R): Metal oxides which react with both acids and bases are amphoteric oxides.

A6. (31/4/1)

Assertion (A): Reaction of Quicklime with water is an exothermic reaction.
Reason (R): Quicklime reacts vigorously with water releasing a large amount of heat.

A7. (31/5/1)

Assertion (A): In ZnO + C → Zn + CO, ZnO undergoes reduction.
Reason (R): Carbon is a reducing agent that reduces ZnO to Zn.

A8. (31/6/1)

Assertion (A): While diluting an acid, one should add water to acid and not acid to water.
Reason (R): Dissolving acid in water is highly exothermic.
